What are you supporting?

Running the Global Racing Series involves a lot of different expenses. This page breaks down the multiple areas covered by the project, why we think they are important and how you can make a difference and shape the Global Racing Series.

Partner events

Up to 15% of the 2024 Global Racing Series budget is dedicated for helping financially Elite Series and Ascend Series event in 2025. Indeed, since the GRS is built with the community events, it is more than normal to support them as much as possible in order to grow collectively.

Within those 15%, the Elite Series events all receive the same help. On their side, the Ascend Series events also all receive an identical (but smaller) help.

Artistic production

Around 10% of our budget is spent on the artistic production. This includes, but not only, the beautiful graphic identity of the Global Racing Series made by Lvyathan with the logo, the multiple templates for the statements, announcements, results used for this website and our social media, the in-game signs, the club design, the stream assets and many other graphic elements.

The creation of 3D assets made to add more immersion into the maps and to incorporate our partners in the best way possible also comes in this category.

In the eventuality of having the GRS All-Star happening offline, the artistic production also includes all the live set assets, a photographer and even a trophy for the winner? Who knows?

Live production

The biggest expenses field is the live production one. It will vary a lot depending on the form the GRS All-Star will take, but it always represents roughly 50% of the total 2024 budget as soon as we reach the Level 2.

This may seem a lot, but it includes a lot of different fields. First of all, of course, the casters. Without them, the event has no emotions. Then, obviously the venue: we need to use a live set for at least an entire day. Also, we need a lot of equipment to use that set: cameras, lights, displays, sound equipment, computers, and maybe even a desk so that our casters don't have to stand up. And, of course, we need few persons to run that set.

Cash prize and expenses

Because we want to reward the best players, the GRS All-Star will feature a cash prize. The qualified players will also have, at least partly, their expenses covered. The goal is to make the event relevant for the players even if they have to travel for far away.

Even if this aspect will only affect a small amount of players, we want to keep the GRS attractive for the best players. That why the cash prize represents almost 20% of the budget.

Other

The last part of the budget is dedicated for all the costs that couldn't fit in the other categories. There is the servers, the website hosting maybe some equipment and last-minute emergencies (like duct tape (we always need duct tape)).
